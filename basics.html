Tennis Basics

Everything you need to start your tennis journey

Getting Started

Tennis is a beginner-friendly sport that anyone can learn with practice. New players focus on learning how to hold the racket, hit the ball consistently, and understand basic rules of play.

Court & Layout

A standard tennis court is divided by a net into two equal halves. There are baseline areas, service boxes, and sidelines that define where the ball must land. Courts can be made of different surfaces such as hard court, clay, or grass, which affect how the ball moves.

Basic Rules

  • Players must hit the ball over the net into the opponent’s court
  • The ball can bounce once before being returned
  • If the ball lands outside the lines, it is out
  • A point is won when the opponent cannot return the ball legally

Scoring System

Tennis has a unique scoring system:

  • 0 = Love
  • 15 = First point
  • 30 = Second point
  • 40 = Third point

If both players reach 40, it becomes deuce. A player must win two points in a row to win the game from deuce.

Singles vs Doubles

In singles, one player competes against another. In doubles, teams of two players compete together. Doubles courts are wider and require teamwork and communication.

Tips for Beginners

  • Focus on consistency before power
  • Keep your eyes on the ball
  • Practice your footwork
  • Start with slow, controlled swings
