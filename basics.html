Tennis Basics

Learn the fundamentals of the game

Getting Started

Tennis is a fast-paced sport that combines athletic ability with strategy. Beginners start by learning how to control the ball, position themselves on the court, and understand how points are played.

How the Game Works

Court Layout

The court is divided into two sides by a net. Players aim to hit the ball into the opponent’s side within the boundary lines.

Objective

The goal is to win points by hitting shots your opponent cannot return legally.

Surfaces

Tennis is played on hard courts, clay courts, and grass, each affecting speed and bounce.

Scoring System

Tennis uses a unique scoring format:

  • 0 = Love
  • 15 = First point
  • 30 = Second point
  • 40 = Third point

If both players reach 40, it is called deuce. A player must win two points in a row to win the game.

Singles vs Doubles

Singles

One player on each side. Requires strong endurance and movement.

Doubles

Two players per team. Focuses more on teamwork, positioning, and communication.

Beginner Tips

  • Start with slow, controlled swings
  • Focus on consistency before power
  • Keep your eyes on the ball
  • Practice footwork regularly
  • Stay relaxed and patient while learning
