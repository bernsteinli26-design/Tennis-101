Tennis Basics

Introduction

Tennis is played between two players (singles) or two teams of two (doubles). The goal is to hit the ball over the net and into the opponent’s court so they cannot return it.

Court & Rules

  • The ball must land inside the court lines
  • Each player gets one bounce to return the ball
  • A point is won when the opponent misses or hits out

Scoring System

0 (love), 15, 30, 40, game. If both players reach 40, it is called deuce. A player must win two points in a row to win the game.

Singles vs Doubles

Singles is one player vs one player. Doubles is two players per team and requires teamwork and communication.

Beginner Tips

  • Focus on consistency before power
  • Keep your eyes on the ball
  • Practice footwork and movement
  • Stay relaxed while playing

History of Tennis

Tennis began in 12th century France as a game called "jeu de paume," where players hit the ball with their hands. Over time, rackets were introduced.

Modern tennis developed in England in the late 1800s and became known as lawn tennis. The first major tournament, Wimbledon, was held in 1877.

Today, tennis is a global sport played professionally and recreationally, with major tournaments known as the Grand Slams.
