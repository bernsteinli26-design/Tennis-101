Tennis Basics

Understanding the foundation of the game

Introduction to Tennis

Tennis is a sport played between two players (singles) or two teams of two players (doubles). The goal is to hit the ball over the net and into the opponent’s court in a way that prevents them from returning it. It combines physical skill, strategy, and mental focus.

Court Layout

A tennis court is divided by a net into two equal sides. Key parts of the court include:

  • Baseline: the back boundary line
  • Service boxes: where serves must land
  • Sidelines: define the width of the court

Courts can be made of different surfaces such as hard court, clay, or grass, each affecting how fast the ball travels and bounces.

Basic Rules

  • The match begins with a serve
  • The ball must go over the net and land within the court boundaries
  • The ball can bounce once before being returned
  • If a player fails to return the ball, the opponent wins the point

Scoring System

Tennis scoring is unique and follows this pattern:

  • 0 = Love
  • 15 = First point
  • 30 = Second point
  • 40 = Third point

If both players reach 40, it becomes deuce. From deuce, a player must win two consecutive points to win the game.

Games make up sets, and sets make up a match.

Singles vs Doubles

Singles: One player on each side. Requires endurance, speed, and strong individual skills.

Doubles: Two players per team. Focuses more on teamwork, positioning, and communication.

Beginner Tips

  • Focus on consistency before trying to hit powerful shots
  • Keep your eyes on the ball at all times
  • Practice footwork to move efficiently
  • Stay relaxed and develop proper technique gradually
